#### Emerging themes (initial)

1. **Convergent case-study skeleton**: Problem → role → process/methods → decisions & tradeoffs → outcomes → learnings (variants across NN/g, Case Study Club, UX Planet, Lovable).
2. **Attention economics**: Short first scan; scannable layout; some tension with “deep case study” depth—resolve with **layered** presentation (summary + detail).
3. **AI-specific**: Portfolios that **show** AI UX (flows, failure states, evaluation, copilot patterns) outperform **claims-only** AI; interactive demos are optional but **evidence must exist in artifacts**.
4. **Healthcare**: Strong exemplars stress **patient/clinician context**, **research**, **ethics/oath**, **visualization of complex data** (see Katie McCurdy); pure **Behance mock** cases are abundant but weaker as **lead** signals unless paired with real constraints narrative.
5. **Leadership**: Sites like **Diego VZ** (org scale, design system, metrics) model **scope**; ensure rubric captures **IC vs lead** explicitly.

#### Questions raised

1. How many public portfolios exist with **oncology-specific** UX + AI? (Hypothesis: **sparse**—may need adjacent **Health** + **AI-prod** synthesis.)
2. For **RAG/chatbot portfolios**, what **failure modes** (wrong answers) would healthcare reviewers penalize? (Follow-up: interview prep / assistant accuracy—not just visual design.)

#### Gaps & limitations (session 1)

- **Survivorship bias**: Curated “best” lists overweight visible designers at brand-name companies.
- **Stale links**: Portfolios churn; retrieval dates and periodic re-check required.
- **Agency vs personal**: Koru, Ramotion, etc. are **agency case studies**—useful for health **content** patterns but different from **individual** lead designer portfolio; mostly excluded from exemplar table unless needed to fill domain gaps.
