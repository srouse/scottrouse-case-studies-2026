# Research Plan: Healthcare AI Lead Designer Portfolio Exemplars

**Audience**: Readers fluent in **AI product design**, **healthcare / high-stakes domains**, and **design craft** (e.g., hiring committee, mentor, or your own bar for quality).

**Constitution**: Aligns with `.researchkit/memory/constitution.md` (citations with retrieval dates for web sources; proportionate confidence; exemplars = informed practice, not proof of causality).

## Research Question

**Primary:**  
What **observable portfolio patterns** (information architecture, case-study depth, AI-specific narrative, healthcare-adjacent trust signals, and visual evidence) appear most often among **strong public portfolios** relevant to a **Lead Designer, AI products** role in **healthcare / oncology**, and how do those patterns compare to **expert guidance** from credible design and health-UX sources?

**Secondary (operational):**  
Which **evaluation questions** best discriminate “generic product portfolio” from “lead-level AI + healthcare-ready portfolio” when reviewing exemplars?

---

## Objectives

- [ ] **Objective 1 (exemplars):** Build a catalog of **≥12** public portfolio sites (personal sites or equivalent long-form case presentation), each tagged for **domain fit** (see rubric below). *Stretch:* prioritize portfolios that touch **clinical / health / regulated** work and/or **AI/ML product** UX; fill remaining slots with **strong AI product** or **healthcare UX** portfolios if direct overlap is scarce—document the gap explicitly.
- [ ] **Objective 2 (structured review):** For each exemplar, complete a **short rubric** (same questions each time) so findings are comparable—see **Exemplar review questions**.
- [ ] **Objective 3 (expert advice):** Collect **≥6** distinct pieces of **guidance** from **higher-credibility** channels (design research orgs, respected practitioners’ essays, health/AI UX talks with clear takeaways—not anonymous listicles). Cite each in `sources.md`.
- [ ] **Objective 4 (synthesis-ready):** Produce notes that support a later synthesis: **recurring patterns**, **outliers**, **conflicts** (e.g., “minimal vs. maximal case studies”), and **limitations** (public portfolios skew toward what people can show; survivorship bias).

---

## Scope

### In scope

- **Portfolio formats:** Personal websites, hosted case studies (e.g., Notion/read.cv) if they function as a **primary portfolio** with multiple projects.
- **Themes:** Case study structure; scannability vs. depth; **AI-specific** sections (model-in-loop, failure modes, evaluation, transparency); **healthcare-adjacent** signals (privacy, safety, clinician workflow, responsible presentation of outcomes).
- **Expert / institutional guidance:** Portfolio and case-study best practices; health UX / human factors primers where they inform **presentation** (not clinical claims).
- **Time:** Emphasis on **current** norms for digital portfolios (roughly last ~5 years); note when a source is dated.

### Out of scope

- **Resume / LinkedIn optimization** as primary artifact (mention only if tied to portfolio narrative).
- **Pure visual galleries** (e.g., Dribbble-only) **without** project narrative—unless used as a single supporting example of what *not* to emulate for this role.
- **Salary negotiation, interview question banks**, or internal employer politics.
- **Medical or regulatory legal advice** (e.g., “FDA says X about your portfolio”—only cite primary docs if genuinely relevant, and label carefully).

### Boundary rationale

The goal is **presentation and signaling** for a **lead AI product designer** in **healthcare**, not general career coaching or clinical/regulatory certification.

## Exemplar domain tags (use in catalog)

Use one **primary** tag per portfolio (pick closest fit; note hybrid in notes):

| Tag | Meaning |
|-----|--------|
| **H+AI** | Clear healthcare / med / life science **and** AI/ML product work |
| **Health** | Healthcare domain; AI not central |
| **AI-prod** | AI/ML product or copilot-style UX; health not central |
| **Product** | Strong product thinking; limited AI or health specificity |
| **Other** | Doesn’t fit—explain |

**Target mix (guidance, not a hard rule):** ≥3 **H+AI** or **Health**, ≥3 **AI-prod**, remainder **Product** or **Other** with justification.

## Exemplar review questions (same for each site)

Answer briefly per portfolio (1–3 sentences or bullets each):

1. **First 30 seconds:** What is the **value proposition** (role, focus, seniority)? Is it obvious?
2. **IA:** Home → work → about/contact; is navigation **obvious** for a busy hiring manager?
3. **Case study arc:** Problem → constraints → role → process → **AI-specific decisions** (if any) → outcome → reflection?
4. **AI credibility:** If AI is claimed, is there **concrete** UI, workflow, metrics, or evaluation—or mostly buzzwords?
5. **Healthcare / high-stakes credibility:** Any **trust** signals (anonymization, safety, clinician workflow, humility about uncertainty)?
6. **Visuals:** Ratio of **real UI / diagrams** vs. decorative imagery? Appropriate for the story?
7. **Verbosity:** **Scannable** (headings, bullets) vs. long prose—does depth match role level?
8. **Lead signals:** Evidence of **scope**, **stakeholders**, **tradeoffs**, **systems**, not only screens?
9. **Overall:** One line—**what works best here** for this target role? One line—**what to avoid**?

Optional numeric gut check (1–5): **Clarity**, **Depth**, **AI specificity**, **Healthcare fit**, **Leadership signal**.

## Search Strategy

### Phase 1: Broad exploration (Days 1–2)

**Goals:** Long list of candidate portfolios + initial expert articles; tag domain fit.

**Keywords / queries (examples):**

- Primary: `UX portfolio case study AI product`, `clinical decision support design portfolio`, `healthcare UX designer portfolio`, `medical UX case study`, `human-centered AI portfolio`
- Secondary: `lead product designer portfolio`, `ML product design case study`, `oncology` + `design` + `portfolio` (may be sparse)
- Alternative: `designer` + `Copilot` / `LLM` + `case study`; `FDA` + `human factors` + `design` (for *advice* sources, not portfolios)

**Where to look:**

- Public portfolio sites from designers at health tech / AI health companies (company attribution in bio—verify public portfolio is theirs).
- Design community roundups (use as **leads only**; verify each site).
- Conference speaker pages (UX healthcare, CHI, ML UX) linking to personal sites.
- Curated lists from reputable design education programs (verify freshness).

**Expert / advice sources (parallel track):**

- Established UX research publishers (e.g., NN/g-style articles on portfolios—verify URL and date).
- Health UX / human factors summaries from **authoritative** bodies or known authors.
- Essays by recognized design leaders on **case study** structure and **AI** product design.

**Phase 1 outputs:** Table in `findings.md` (once execution starts) with **≥20** candidates; narrow to **≥12** reviewed in depth.

---

### Phase 2: Deep investigation (Days 3–5)

**Goals:** Complete rubric for **≥12** portfolios; expand `sources.md` with every exemplar URL + access date + short descriptor.

**Focus areas:**

- **Pattern mining:** Which sections recur among the strongest **H+AI / Health / AI-prod** tags?
- **Gap analysis:** If **H+AI** exemplars are rare, document that limitation and infer from **Health** + **AI-prod** separately.
- **Advice crosswalk:** Map expert recommendations to exemplars (which sites exemplify which advice).

**Verification (per constitution):**

- **Portfolio claims** about outcomes: treat as **self-reported**; note uncertainty.
- **“Best practice”** claims: prefer **2+** independent expert sources or one authoritative source + illustration from exemplars.
- **Conflicting** design advice (e.g., minimal site vs. rich case studies): document tradeoffs by **audience** (recruiter skim vs. design director deep read).

---

### Phase 3: Cross-verification (Days 6–7)

**Goals:** Stable list of **patterns** vs. **one-offs**; explicit limitations.

**Tasks:**

- Revisit **2–3** exemplars that seemed strongest—confirm notes; fix inconsistencies.
- Ensure **≥6** expert/advice sources** are cited and summarized.
- List **open questions** (e.g., “Need more oncology-specific public portfolios—may require private redacted case instead”).
- Prepare **inputs for synthesis** (`/researchkit_synthesize`): pattern list, conflicts, limitations, recommended portfolio outline for *your* build (optional follow-on).
